## What We're Building

A daily workflow for boosting likes on TikTok comments to force them to the top of comment sections on viral rap fan page videos. The comment itself acts as advertising — everyone who watches the viral video sees our message prominently displayed as the top comment.

**Core loop:**
1. Monitor target rap fan pages for new video posts
2. Drop a comment within the first 10 minutes of a video going up
3. Order 3-5K comment likes via an SMM panel (drip-fed over 2-4 hours)
4. If the video is blowing up, add another 3-5K likes to cement the top position
5. Track results (likes ordered, likes retained, video views)

**This is NOT about driving followers or profile clicks.** The goal is purely visibility — making the comment loud, visible, and seen by everyone watching the video.

## Why This Approach

**Chosen approach: Hybrid (Mid-Tier SMM Panel + Timing Strategy)**

We evaluated three approaches:

| Approach | Monthly Cost (daily use) | Effectiveness | Complexity |
|----------|------------------------|---------------|------------|
| Basic SMM Panel (no timing) | $900/mo | Moderate — high drop rates, needs 20K+ likes | Low |
| Premium Service (no timing) | $3,000/mo | High — likes stick, but expensive | Low |
| **Hybrid (mid-tier + timing)** | **$300-720/mo** | **High — timing reduces likes needed** | **Medium** |

**Why Hybrid wins:**
- Commenting early (first 10 min) means you need far fewer bought likes to reach top position
- TikTok's algorithm treats early engagement as organic signal
- 5-8K well-timed likes beats 20K late likes
- Cost is 3-4x lower than premium at the same effectiveness
- Daily use makes the timing habit second nature

### 3. Campaign burst timing
Heavy pushes around specific moments (new music drops, viral moments, trending topics) with daily baseline activity. Not constant — intensity scales with opportunity.

### 4. Drip-feed delivery
Likes should be delivered gradually over 2-4 hours, not dumped all at once. Gradual delivery looks more natural and is harder for TikTok to detect.

### 5. Test-first approach
Before committing to daily use: test 2-3 different panels with small orders (500-1K likes). Measure drop rate after 72 hours. Pick the panel with the best retention.

## Daily Workflow

**Setup (one-time):**
- Create 2-3 dedicated TikTok comment accounts (different emails, phones, unique profiles)
- Identify 5-10 target rap fan pages that consistently post viral content (100K+ views)
- Turn on TikTok post notifications for all target pages
- Sign up for 2-3 SMM panels to test
- Create a tracking spreadsheet

**Daily operation:**
1. Watch for notifications from target pages
2. When a new video drops, open it and comment within 10 minutes
3. Copy the comment URL
4. Go to SMM panel → order 3-5K TikTok comment likes → drip-feed mode
5. Check back 2-3 hours later:
   - Video blowing up? Add 3-5K more likes
   - Video flopping? Don't waste more money
6. Log in spreadsheet: date, video URL, comment text, likes ordered, likes retained (check at 72 hours)

**Weekly review:**
- Check 72-hour retention rates across all comments
- Calculate cost per sustained top comment position
- Adjust panel choice or order sizes based on data

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Likes get purged by TikTok | Medium | Low — order more or accept lower count | Use mid-tier panels, drip-feed delivery, track drop rates |
| Comment account gets banned | Low | Low — use dedicated accounts, not main ones | Keep 2-3 comment accounts ready as backups |
| Video creator deletes comment | Medium | Low — lost the cost of likes on that comment | Only boost comments on pages that don't heavily moderate |
| TikTok changes algorithm | Low | Medium — top comment logic could change | Monitor if the strategy stops working, adapt |
| Panel goes offline / scams | Low | Low — small amounts at risk | Don't pre-load large balances, use multiple panels |

**Important:** TikTok does NOT typically ban the *recipient* of fake likes — they ban the bot accounts doing the liking. Your comment account risk is low, but not zero.
