## 1. Tiered Pricing Models

### What Successful Agencies Use

The industry has converged on **3-tier structures** as the standard. Here is how they map to a clipping agency:

| Tier | Typical Agency Range | Post Equivalent | What Changes Between Tiers |
|------|---------------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Starter / Bronze** | $1,500-$3,500/mo | $5,000-$8,000/mo | 2 platforms, 10-15 clips/day, monthly report, email support |
| **Growth / Silver** | $3,500-$7,500/mo | $10,000-$15,000/mo | 4 platforms, 15-20 clips/day, bi-weekly report + mid-month check-in, priority support |
| **Enterprise / Gold** | $7,500-$20,000+/mo | $15,000-$25,000/mo | 4 platforms, 20-30 clips/day, weekly report, reactive/same-day clips, strategy calls, dedicated sound curation |

**Per-platform pricing** is common in broader social media management but less relevant for clipping agencies where the core work (identifying moments, generating clips) is platform-agnostic. The variable cost is in distribution/scheduling. Consider charging a **base rate + per-platform add-on**:

- Base (content creation + 2 platforms): $8,000/mo
- Each additional platform: +$2,000-$3,000/mo
- All 4 platforms (TikTok, IG, YouTube Shorts, X): $14,000-$17,000/mo

**Volume-based pricing** (per-clip) is used at the low end of the market:

- Freelance clippers: $15-$90 per clip
- AI-assisted clip shops: $5-$25 per clip
- Performance-based clipping networks (Clip by Max Peterson, Clipping Culture): $0.10 per 1,000 views, or $300-$1,500 per 1 million views

Post should **not** price per-clip. Retainers are superior for revenue stability, client retention, and perceived value.

**Performance-based bonuses** layered on top of retainers:

- Base retainer + 5-10% bonus when clips exceed a monthly view threshold (e.g., 10M+ views)
- Base retainer + $500-$1,000 bonus per viral clip (>1M views)
- Revenue share on monetized views: 10-30% of attributed revenue

**Recommended structure for Post:**

## 2. Value-Based Pricing: Justifying $10-20k vs $5k

### What Separates Premium from Budget

The difference between a $5k/mo clipping service and a $15k/mo clipping service is **not more clips** -- it is the quality of judgment, speed, and strategic integration.

**What justifies premium pricing ($10-20k):**

1. **Taste and editorial judgment** -- Sean's ability to identify genuinely viral moments is the core differentiator. AI tools alone (Opus Clip, etc.) produce "decent filler content but rarely go viral" (per the brainstorm doc). This human curation layer is what clients pay for.

2. **Speed/reactivity** -- Same-day clips when something trends. Low-end services operate on 48-72 hour turnaround. Reactivity is a premium feature.

3. **Multi-account distribution strategy** -- Not just making clips but distributing them across 10-30+ accounts with unique captions, staggered timing, and per-platform optimization. This is the "flooding the zone" strategy that NPR reported major talent agencies like UTA now use.

4. **Platform-specific optimization** -- Trending sounds on TikTok/IG, royalty-free on YouTube/X, unique captions per account, compliance with per-platform algorithm rules. A $5k service posts the same clip everywhere.

5. **Risk management** -- DMCA handling, copyright clearance, account ban mitigation, content compliance. Cheaper services do not handle this.

6. **Strategic partnership** -- Monthly/bi-weekly calls, content strategy, posting cadence optimization, trend monitoring. The client gets a creative director, not just an editor.

**What budget services ($2-5k) look like:**

- AI-generated clips with minimal human review
- Same clip posted across platforms with identical captions
- No sound curation or trending audio
- Weekly or slower turnaround
- No reactive capability
- Template reports or no reporting
- Usually offshore labor or pure AI output

**Pricing psychology:**

- The Wayfront retainer pricing guide recommends a **setup fee of 50-100% of the first month's retainer** to cover onboarding. For Post, a $5,000-$10,000 onboarding fee is justifiable given the pilot batch process, brand guideline intake, and content strategy call.
- Anchor the client's perception: "Creators spend $50K/month on agencies" (documented in industry reporting). At $10-20k, Post is mid-market, not premium.
- Frame pricing around **ROI**: If a creator earns $100k+/month from their social presence, $10-20k for the system that drives discoverability is 10-20% of revenue -- a standard marketing spend ratio.

### Is This Sustainable?

**Yes, for these reasons:**

1. **The rate reflects leverage, not overcharging.** Sean is not selling hours -- he is selling a system (AI tools + taste + distribution infrastructure). The client pays for the output (15-20 clips/day across 30+ accounts), not the input (Sean's time). This is textbook value-based pricing.

2. **Comparable high-leverage operators exist.** Solopreneur agency owners operating with AI tools and productized services report 40-60% profit margins and effective rates of $300-$500+/hr, per the Ken Yarmosh solopreneur agency model. The key is keeping overhead near zero (which Post does at $121/mo in tools).

3. **The ceiling is client ROI, not market rates.** If a streamer with 5M followers earns $50k-$500k/month from their social media presence, $10-20k for the engine that drives their short-form discoverability is a small percentage of revenue. As long as Sean's service demonstrably contributes to the client's growth, the pricing holds.

4. **The risk is concentration, not rate.** At 1-2 clients, one churn event is catastrophic. At 3-4 clients ($30-80k/mo revenue), the effective hourly rate decreases as time increases, but total revenue provides a buffer. The sustainable target is **3-4 clients at $10-15k each = $30-60k/mo on 3-5 hours/day of work**.

## 6. Referral Incentives

### What Works for Agencies

| Referral Structure | How It Works | Pros | Cons |
|-------------------|-------------|------|------|
| **Flat cash bonus** | $1,000-$2,500 per referred client who signs | Simple, motivating | One-time cost, no ongoing alignment |
| **First-month discount** | Referrer gets 10-20% off next month; new client gets 10% off first month | Both parties benefit | Revenue hit on two accounts simultaneously |
| **Recurring commission** | 5-10% of referred client's retainer for 6-12 months | Strong ongoing incentive | Can get expensive at $10-20k retainers |
| **Service credit** | $500-$1,000 service credit (extra clips, strategy call, etc.) | Zero cash outlay | Less motivating than cash |
| **Tiered recognition** | Escalating rewards: 1 referral = $1,000; 3 referrals = $5,000; 5+ = free month | Gamifies referrals | Complex to administer |

**Recommended for Post:**

Given that Post's clients are high-value ($10-20k/mo), a simple and generous referral program works best:

- **Referrer:** $2,000 credit toward next month's retainer per successful referral (client who signs and pays first invoice)
- **Referred client:** First month at 10% discount ($1,000-$2,000 off)
- **Total cost per referral:** $3,000-$4,000 -- easily justified against a $300k+ LTV

**Important:** 90% of people trust word-of-mouth over other advertising. In the creator/streamer community, referrals between artists and their managers are the primary acquisition channel. The existing brainstorm doc confirms "Clients are already coming through referrals; the site reinforces credibility, it doesn't generate leads." Formalizing this with a referral program converts organic word-of-mouth into a predictable acquisition channel.

**Referral program reduces customer acquisition cost (CAC) by up to 13%** according to industry data.

## 7. Annual vs. Monthly Contracts

### Industry Standards

| Contract Length | Typical Discount | When to Use | Risk |
|----------------|-----------------|-------------|------|
| **Month-to-month** | No discount (full rate) | Trial clients, first 3 months | High churn risk |
| **3-month commitment** | 0-5% discount | New clients post-pilot | Moderate churn risk |
| **6-month commitment** | 5-10% discount | Standard for music artists (album cycles) | Low churn risk |
| **12-month commitment** | 10-15% discount (up to 25% in some agencies) | Established clients, streamers, podcasters | Lowest churn risk |

**Industry data:** Annual plans offer 15-25% discounts compared to monthly billing. Most agencies use 3-6 month starter agreements, then transition to 12-month renewals.

**Recommended for Post:**

| Term | Pricing | Discount | Conditions |
|------|---------|----------|------------|
| **Month-to-month** | Full rate ($10-20k) | None | Available after 3-month minimum |
| **6-month contract** | 5% off | $500-$1,000/mo savings | Recommended for music artists |
| **12-month contract** | 10% off | $1,000-$2,000/mo savings | Recommended for streamers/podcasters |
| **First 3 months** | Full rate, no discount | — | Mandatory minimum term for all new clients |

**Why a 3-month minimum:** The existing plan's 14-day onboarding + pilot batch process means it takes 2-4 weeks before the client sees full production value. A 1-month contract would allow cancellation before the system demonstrates results. Three months gives enough time for the algorithm to reward consistent posting and for view counts to compound.

**Long-term contracts (6-12 months) reduce churn from 42% (project-based) to 18% (retainer-based)** per industry benchmarks. They also stabilize revenue forecasting and justify deeper investment in each client's strategy.

## 8. Payment Terms

### Industry Standards

| Term | Prevalence | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Prepaid (due before service month)** | Growing standard for agencies | Best for Post -- eliminates collections risk |
| **Net 7** | Common for retainers | Good balance of cash flow and client flexibility |
| **Net 15** | Less common | Acceptable if client requires it |
| **Net 30** | ~60% of B2B transactions | Standard but problematic for solo operators (cash flow risk) |
| **Net 60** | Enterprise clients only | Avoid unless paired with 40-50% upfront deposit |

### Deposits and Onboarding Fees

| Fee Type | Amount | When Collected | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Setup/onboarding fee** | 50-100% of monthly retainer ($5,000-$20,000) | Before work begins | Covers onboarding, brand intake, pilot batch, account setup |
| **First month prepayment** | Full first month's retainer | Before work begins | Cash flow security, demonstrates client commitment |
| **Security deposit** | 1 month's retainer (optional) | At contract signing | Protects against sudden cancellation; refundable at end of term |

**Key data point:** When agencies switch from Net 30 to 50% upfront deposit models, average collection time drops from 47 days to 22 days with zero client losses.

**Recommended for Post:**

1. **Onboarding fee:** $5,000 one-time (covers pilot batch process, brand intake, account setup). Credited toward first month if client signs 6+ month contract.
2. **Payment timing:** Prepaid. Invoice sent on the 1st, due within 7 days, for the upcoming month of service.
3. **Late payment:** 1.5%/month penalty. 14-day grace period. Service pauses on Day 22 if unpaid. (Already in the existing plan.)
4. **Preferred payment method:** Stripe auto-pay (recurring subscription). Reduces friction and eliminates collections work entirely.
5. **Never offer Net 30 or Net 60.** As a solo operator, Sean cannot afford 30-60 day cash flow gaps. The clients Post serves (creators earning $50k-$500k+/mo) have no legitimate reason to delay payment.

## 9. Scaling Pricing (When Sean Hires Help)

### Margin Targets

| Business Stage | Target Net Margin | Rationale |
|---------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Solo (current)** | 99%+ ($121/mo tools on $10-20k revenue) | All revenue is profit minus tools |
| **Solo + 1 part-time contractor** | 70-80% | Pay contractor $2-4k/mo for scheduling/captions |
| **Solo + 2 contractors** | 60-70% | Add a second for clip review or additional clients |
| **Small team (3-4 people)** | 50-60% | Solopreneur agency model benchmark |
| **Traditional agency benchmark** | 15-25% net margin | For comparison only -- Post should never drop this low |

**Industry context:**

- Solopreneur agencies maintain **40-60% profit margins** vs. 10-20% for traditional agencies (Ken Yarmosh model)
- 8-figure agencies maintain 25-32% margins; 7-figure agencies average 18-22% (Predictable Profits 2025 benchmark)
- Experts recommend **55% delivery margin** on P&L and **70% delivery margin** on projects
- Staffing agency markups range 25-100%, with gross margins of 14-41%

### How to Adjust Pricing When Scaling

**Option A: Keep prices the same, absorb contractor cost (recommended at first)**

| Scale | Revenue | Contractor Cost | Tool Cost | Net Margin |
|-------|---------|----------------|-----------|------------|
| 3 clients, solo | $30-45k/mo | $0 | ~$190 | 99% |
| 4 clients, +1 contractor | $40-60k/mo | $3,000-$4,000 | ~$260 | 89-93% |
| 6 clients, +2 contractors | $60-90k/mo | $6,000-$8,000 | ~$350 | 87-91% |

**Option B: Raise prices as you scale (recommended at 5+ clients)**

- Justify price increases with enhanced deliverables: faster turnaround, more clips, added strategy calls
- New clients come in at higher rates; existing clients get a 6-12 month price lock
- Annual price increase of 5-10% for renewing clients is standard

**Option C: Create a lower tier served by contractors, premium tier served by Sean**

- "Core" tier ($5,000-$8,000/mo): Contractor-produced clips, Sean reviews. Lower touch.
- "Elite" tier ($15,000-$25,000/mo): Sean personally involved in all hero clips, strategy calls, reactive clips.
- This naturally segments the market and protects Sean's time for highest-value clients.

### Who to Hire First

Based on the existing plan's bottleneck analysis (Later scheduling at 25-35 min/client is the first constraint):

1. **First hire: Part-time scheduler/caption writer** ($2,000-$4,000/mo, ~15-20 hrs/week). Handles Later uploads, unique captions, hashtags, scheduling. Sean still reviews clips and picks hero moments. Saves 25-35 min/client/day.

2. **Second hire: Clip reviewer/QA** ($2,000-$3,000/mo, ~10-15 hrs/week). Scans filler clips, flags issues, manages DMCA responses. Sean focuses only on hero clips.

3. **Critical rule:** Never let margin drop below 50% when hiring. If a contractor costs $4,000/mo, they must free up enough of Sean's time to serve at least one additional client ($10,000+ in revenue).
